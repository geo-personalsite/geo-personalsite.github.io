Outer Banks

One of the best stargazing spots on the East Coast


For people in Virginia, this is one of the easiest places to witness the Milky Way. During the day, you can explore the small town, relax on the beach, explore a "desert-like" place, eat some great seafood and catch the sunset. At night, you can head out to the beach and see the Milky Way.

I've typically only done this as an "one-night" trip with friends. I go camping at Oregon Inlet Campground, where it's around $28 per night. Make sure to bring a tent! They have grills, so you can grill some food too.

For best star viewing

For stargazing, it is best to go during clear skies and when it's a few days before or after there is a new moon. You can check the moon phases here.

At Oregon Inlet Campground, you can walk to the beach and see stars there. Also be careful, there might be lots of mosquitos there.

For Photographers interested in astrophotography

Check out this YouTube tutorial. I typically used a Canon 5D Mark II, with a 35mm and a tripod. My typical settings for astrophotography are in manual mode f/2, ISO 3200, 15" exposure, manual focus to infinity.
