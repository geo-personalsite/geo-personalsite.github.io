Iceland

The Land of Fire and Ice

March 4 - 9, 2018
Full Itinerary

Iceland was definitely one of my favorite places I have ever been. It's a land of volcanoes, glaciers, snow, wind, no trees and really nice views. If you happen to go during the right time of year, you can also catch the Northern Lights. Shoutout to my friends Brad Lanza, Cindy Yu and Sam Miller for coming on this trip and making it a blast!

Travel notes

Use AirBnb or Booking.com to get housing! It is pretty cheap to get housing in Iceland. If you happen to be in this area, we stayed in an incredible homey cottage, and it had INCREDIBLE views.

We flew from BWI -> KEF using WOWAIR airlines. However, WOWAIR is out of business and no longer in service. IcelandAir can still provide roundtrip travel to Iceland. In terms of traveling around Iceland, we rented a car at the airport, and it was relatively cheap if you split with friends.

The main highway around the entire island of Iceland is known as the Golden Circle, and the roads are incredibly well maintained. If you have lots of time, a popular choice of traveling through Iceland is driving the entire Golden Circle. We didn't have enough time due to spring break, so we just did a section of the Golden Circle, shown in the map below.

A recommendation: I suggest using the Google Maps and downloading an "offline" map of Iceland. Even if you don't have service or wifi you can still use the "offline" map to navigate through Iceland. Here's a YouTube Tutorial.



Food notes

Food in Iceland is EXPENSIVE. We brought snacks from USA to save money, and we only ate dinners in restaurants. Most meals in Iceland were at least $20. There are plenty of grocery stores in Iceland as well, if you want PB&J sandwiches, fruits and/or Icelandic snacks as snacks or lunch.
